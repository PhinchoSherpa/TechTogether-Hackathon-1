I knew as a kid I always wanted to be a journalist ever since I was a kid. Writing stories about the world always fascinated me, but I never had the confidence that I would succeed, that is until I signed up to shadow a mentor at Future Vision. From my experiences shadowing a real journalist, I realized: 'Hey, this is pretty doable. I can do this. I'm ready for this.' From there, I honed my writing more and more throughout college and now I'm a full fledged journalist employed at a publication.

Mary Teol

Future Vision helped provide me a pathway to succeed. I was a buisness major in college and I was looking for internships over the summer. All my friends seemed to getting one left and right, but I could never find one. In the middle of that panic, I decided to attend a networking event hosted by Future Vision. In the middle of that event, I met the owner of a local buisness firm. We talked for a bit, and afterwards he offered me a internship, saying he respected my passion and drive. Such possibilities could've never happened without Future Vision.

George Hanweiz

When I was 16, I had absolute no idea what I was going to do for a living. I found about Future Vision from some of my friends, and I had nothing better to do so I signed up for a electrical engineering workshop. Little did I know how much that would change my life. Seeing all those electronics interact with other was simply a transformative moment, and from that point onwards I was absorbed into the world of engineering. Now I'm enrolled in college studying electrical engineering and learning more about my passions.
